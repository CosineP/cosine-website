Flip

Your character is that blue “R,” and your goal is to make it to the green “R.” Clicking on one of those black bars reflects your character over that axis. (The game is embedded to the left.)

This is an interesting game, because it asks the player to find the solution Coxeter group, given certain possible reflections. I had to learn the mathematics of reflections and present them without sounding like math!

One difficult thing is calculating the "R"s' positions. All of the reflections are calculated using Matrix multiplication. It was a challenge to find a way to calculate these matrices based on the line's beginning and end.

Subreddit Classifier

Subreddit Classifier downloads images from the social media site reddit. Reddit has subfora (“subreddits”) that focus on specific topics. I created a Convolutional Neural Network that attempts to classify what subreddit an image should belong to.

The project, when it had all of its data, was able to classify certain subreddits with 90% accuracy (unfortunately the >20GB of data were lost in a HDD failure)

Pictured is the classifications made (with less data and training - my original data was lost in a hard drive failure) of the subreddit “animewallpaper” vs all combined subreddits (“all”).

As you can see, /r/animewallpaper receives very few false positives. (In fact, the network is trained for this, as false-positives are more destructive in my goals than false-negatives).

Entropy

I made this game for Ludum Dare, a "game jam" in which people make games in 48 hours about a specific theme, and share them with each other. This theme was One Room.

My idea was to make a dungeon crawler where, instead of advancing to other rooms, you advance to different energy levels of the same room. The higher energy levels are more difficult to survive in.

I really enjoyed what I made, so I continued to work on it. I fixed some bugs and made it support touch fully, so that it could be used on mobile phones.

It's embedded to the left. Use the arrow keys to move around, and click in a direction to shoot. When you're ready, go to one of those blue portals and hold E to advance to the next energy level.

Swarm

I made this game for Ludum Dare as well; this theme was You are the enemy. I wanted to make a game where you don't have the "hero's advantage" you have in many games - you're just another grunt, more likely to die than anyone else. To do this, I made it so that you were amongst a huge swarm of enemies, and upon dying your consciousness transferred to another member of the swarm. This made balance interesting, because you play a small - but still significant! - part in the outcome of the game.

For this game I wanted to focus on making the game feel "juicy." So I focused on making the graphics fun (I used pastel colors of pink and cyan), and added some fun sound effects. I especially liked my way of making subtle screenshot to make your shots feel powerful.

It's embedded to the left. Use the keys WSAD as arrow keys to move around, and click in a direction to shoot. You must kill the blue square-ish thing (a Triflapper)
